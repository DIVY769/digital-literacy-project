## General Cyber Safety Tips for College Students

1. **Never share your UPI PIN or OTP with anyone** – No bank, government body, or
   app will ever ask for your PIN over call, SMS, or chat. If someone asks, it is a scam.

2. **Understand that QR codes are for sending money, not receiving** – Scanning
   a QR code to "receive" a payment is impossible. Any request to scan a QR code
   to receive money is always fraudulent.

3. **Verify before you trust** – If you receive a call from someone claiming to be
   a bank or company, hang up and call the official customer care number from the
   bank's official website to verify.

4. **Do not click unknown links in SMS or WhatsApp** – Fraudulent payment links
   are often disguised as official bank or government URLs. Check the full URL
   carefully before opening.

5. **Enable transaction alerts on your bank account** – Set up SMS and email
   notifications for every transaction so you detect unauthorized activity immediately.

6. **Lock your UPI app with biometrics or a separate PIN** – If your phone is
   lost or stolen, this prevents immediate access to your payment apps.

7. **Be cautious on second-hand selling platforms** – Buyers who ask for your
   UPI ID and then say "I sent money, please confirm with OTP" are attempting fraud.
   Never share OTPs for incoming payments.

8. **Report suspicious activity immediately** – Do not wait to see if the situation
   resolves itself. Quick action can sometimes freeze transactions before funds are lost.

---

## UPI-Specific Safety Tips

9. **Only use official apps** – Use BHIM, Google Pay, PhonePe, or your bank's
   official app from verified sources (Google Play Store / Apple App Store only).

10. **Avoid using UPI on public Wi-Fi** – Public networks can be monitored.
    Always use mobile data when making financial transactions.
