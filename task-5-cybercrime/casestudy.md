## What Is UPI Payment Fraud?

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a type of financial cybercrime where
attackers trick individuals into sending money or revealing sensitive payment
credentials — such as UPI PINs or OTPs — through social engineering, fake apps,
or deceptive links.

---

## How It Typically Happens – Step by Step

1. **Initial Contact:** The victim receives a call, SMS, or WhatsApp message from
   someone posing as a bank official, government representative, or online seller.

2. **Creating Urgency:** The attacker claims there is an issue with the victim's
   account — such as a KYC update pending, a prize to collect, or a failed
   transaction to reverse.

3. **The Deceptive Request:** The victim is asked to share their UPI PIN, OTP,
   or to scan a QR code / click a link "to receive" money or verify their account.

4. **The Trap:** Scanning the QR code or entering the PIN does not receive money —
   it authorizes a payment out of the victim's account.

5. **Money Is Transferred:** Within seconds, the victim's balance is debited.
   The attacker disconnects, and the funds are moved across multiple accounts
   to avoid tracing.

---

## Who Is Usually Targeted?

- College students new to digital payments
- Elderly individuals unfamiliar with UPI systems
- People selling items on second-hand platforms (OLX, Facebook Marketplace)
- Anyone receiving unexpected "prize" or "refund" messages

---

## Consequences

- Immediate financial loss (sometimes entire savings)
- Emotional distress, anxiety, and loss of trust in digital platforms
- In severe cases, identity theft if additional personal data was shared
- Difficulty recovering funds — UPI transactions are near-instant and hard to reverse
