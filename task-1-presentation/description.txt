TOOL USED: Canva (canva.com)

WHAT THE INFOGRAPHIC SHOWS:
The infographic is titled "Be Smart Online – A Digital Literacy Guide for Students."
It covers three key topics:

1. What Digital Literacy Is:
   Digital literacy means being able to use digital tools effectively, safely, and
   responsibly. It is not just about knowing how to operate a device or app — it is
   about understanding the impact of your digital actions on yourself and others.

2. Safe Internet Practices:
   - Never share passwords or OTPs with anyone
   - Always verify links before clicking
   - Use strong, unique passwords for each platform
   - Enable two-factor authentication wherever possible

3. Professional Online Presence:
   - Keep your LinkedIn and GitHub profiles updated and accurate
   - Use a professional photo and college email for sign-ups
   - Think before you post — your digital footprint is permanent

DESIGN CHOICES:
The infographic uses a clean blue and white color scheme to look professional and
readable. Icons were used for each section to make it visually engaging for students.
The layout is single-column to keep the reading flow simple.

WHAT I FOUND INTERESTING:
I found it interesting that Canva offers so many templates specifically for educational
infographics. The hardest part was deciding what to include — there was too much
important information to fit on one page, so I had to prioritize the most relevant points
for first-year students.
