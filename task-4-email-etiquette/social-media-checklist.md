# Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students

**Student:** Divy Jindal | **Reg No:** 25BCE10769

---

## ✅ DO's

1. **Keep your profiles professional** – Use your real name and a clear profile photo on platforms like LinkedIn and GitHub. Recruiters and faculty do look at these.

2. **Think before you post** – Ask yourself: "Would I be comfortable if my professor or future employer saw this?" If not, do not post it.

3. **Use privacy settings** – Regularly review who can see your posts and personal information on platforms like Instagram and Facebook.

4. **Credit original creators** – If you share someone else's content, idea, or work, always give them proper credit. This applies to memes, articles, and code.

5. **Report harmful content** – If you encounter cyberbullying, misinformation, or abusive content, use the platform's reporting tools. Do not ignore it.

---

## ❌ DON'Ts

1. **Don't share personal information publicly** – Avoid posting your phone number, home address, college ID, or daily schedule on public-facing profiles.

2. **Don't engage in arguments or spread rumors** – Online conflicts escalate quickly and leave a permanent record. Walk away from toxic interactions.

3. **Don't post during exams or sensitive periods** – Sharing that you are "free" or "bored during exams" can send the wrong signal to peers and academic authorities.

4. **Don't exaggerate or misrepresent yourself** – Do not claim certifications, skills, or achievements you do not have. Digital platforms are increasingly verified.

5. **Don't click suspicious links shared on social media** – Scam links circulate frequently through WhatsApp groups and Instagram DMs. Always verify the source before clicking.
